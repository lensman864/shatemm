In Nigeria, access to affordable housing has largely remained an unfulfilled dream to the vast majority most especially, the middle and the lower class of the society due to the challenges of housing in Ngeria.

Nigeria with a population of about 174 million people is currently facing a national housing deficit of about 17 million units. In 1991, the Nigeria hous - ing deficit was at 7 million, it has since increased from 7 million in 1991, to 12 million in 2007, 14 million in 2010 and currently 17 million units.

The implications of this very high housing deficit is that tenants in rented apartments pay as high as 60% of their average disposable income far higher than the 20-30% recommended by the United Nations.

Also, experts believe that it is only 10% of those who desire owning a home in Nigeria can afford it, either by way of purchase or personal construction as against.

TURNING THE DIRT UNDER OUR FEET INTO AMAZING STRUCTURES

The soil is mixed with sand if needed and 4-8% cement or lime (stabilizer). Water is added to hydrate the stabilizer and all the ingredients are mixed using shovels and rakes. This mixing process is easily automated with a mixing machine, but in many areas job creation trumps the desire for more automated systems. The mixed soil is then loaded into the earthblockmachine using buckets
